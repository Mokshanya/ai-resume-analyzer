100% Algorithmic Emulation

Human recruiters don't throw away your resume—an ATS algorithm does. Before a human ever previews your file, enterprise software must successfully extract your exact career history.

We rigorously replicate the extraction logic used by standard systems like Workday, Taleo, and Greenhouse. Our Python backend tests your document's structural integrity so your layout doesn't crash the parser.

  • Validates legacy and modern PDF formats
  • Tests multi-column layout stripping
  • Identifies hidden tables causing algorithmic failure

Semantic Keyword Logic

Modern tracking systems don't just "Ctrl+F" your resume for keywords. They evaluate the contextual density of those skills within your actual project descriptions.

We dynamically calculate your keyword match-rate based on the targeted tech role. If you claim to be a "React Developer" but only mention "React" once in a standalone skills list, the ATS algorithm automatically flags your application as a low-relevance match.

  • Generates targeted SaaS and Enterprise keyword maps
  • Analyzes syntactic proximity in bullet points
  • Prevents "keyword stuffing" penalties

Action-Driven Replacements

A strong ATS profile doesn't just list responsibilities—it proves continuous impact. Using passive verbs like "Handled" or "Responsible for" signals a lack of leadership and sinks your profile scoring tier.

Our deep-learning model actively highlights every weak verb in your resume and automatically generates high-impact, metric-driven rewrites specifically curated to capture the attention of technical and SaaS recruiters instantly.

  • Flags over 500 passive verbs & filler words
  • Suggests active, metric-focused replacements
  • Aligns tone with top Fortune 500 performers
